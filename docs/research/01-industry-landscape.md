# 01 — Industry Landscape: Food-Waste & Pantry Apps

**Author:** Research pass, accessed 2026-05-03
**Scope:** Consumer-facing UK-relevant comparables for FridgeCheck — pantry tracking, expiry alerts, fridge management, household food-waste reduction.

> All claims with a link were verified live on 2026-05-03 via WebSearch / WebFetch. Anything I could not verify is flagged `[low confidence]`.

---

## 1. Market context (UK, 2026)

- The total cost of UK household food waste is approximately **£17 billion per year, or about £1,000 a year for a household of four** ([WRAP, 2026 press release](https://www.wrap.ngo/media-centre/press-releases/sunday-15-march-average-uk-household-four-will-have-already-wasted), accessed 2026-05-03). FridgeCheck's £730/year hook traces to WRAP 2018 — same source Kitche cites on its homepage ([kitche.co](https://kitche.co/), accessed 2026-05-03).
- 4.4 million tonnes of edible food is binned from UK homes annually, generating 16 Mt CO₂e ([WRAP](https://www.wrap.ngo/media-centre/press-releases/sunday-15-march-average-uk-household-four-will-have-already-wasted), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Food Waste Action Week 2026** ran 9–15 March under the slogan "Make Your Food Go Fuuuuuuuurther" — WRAP's annual peak campaign window for category attention ([WRAP toolkit](https://www.wrap.ngo/resources/campaign-assets/food-waste-action-week-2026-toolkit-assets), accessed 2026-05-03).
- Weekly food-waste collections are rolling out across England in 2026 under the Simpler Recycling reforms — WRAP themselves warn this could "normalise waste rather than prevent it", which is a tailwind for prevention-focused tools like FridgeCheck ([Resource Media](https://resourcemedia.eco/article/wrap-warns-food-waste-collections-could-normalise-waste-rather-than-prevent-it), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Tesco** launched a large-scale colleague trial in April 2026 of an AI assistant inside its Clubcard app for meal planning, basket-building, and using leftover ingredients — rolling out to all customers later in 2026, built with Tomoro AI / OpenAI ([The Grocer](https://www.thegrocer.co.uk/news/tesco-launches-meal-planning-basket-building-in-app-ai-assistant/717474.article), [Tesco PLC](https://www.tescoplc.com/tesco-launches-large-scale-colleague-trial-of-new-ai-assistant-designed-to-lighten-the-load-for-customers/), accessed 2026-05-03). This is the single biggest competitive threat to AI-assisted "use what you have" features in the UK market.
- Samsung Family Hub fridges and LG Signature Smart InstaView gained CES 2026 upgrades for in-fridge inventory recognition (Bixby + Gemini for Samsung; on-device LLM for LG) ([The Outpost](https://theoutpost.ai/news-story/samsung-smart-fridges-gain-voice-control-and-ai-powered-food-recognition-at-ces-2026-22730/), accessed 2026-05-03). High-end hardware play, low penetration in average UK households for now.

## 3. Per-competitor deep dive

### 3.1 Kitche
- **URL:** <https://kitche.co/>
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android, plus web platform.
- **Description:** UK-built household food-waste app with a "Kitche it, don't ditch it" tagline. Receipt-scan adds items, fires expiry reminders, and surfaces recipes filtered by what's in the pantry. Includes voice + barcode entry, a personal "food-waste cost vs national average" graph, and gamified challenges/digital rewards ([Kitche.co](https://kitche.co/), [Sustainable Business Magazine relaunch piece](https://sustainablebusinessmagazine.net/technology/kitche-a-uk-award-winning-home-food-waste-app-and-platform-has-just-announced-a-re-launch-ofits-app-with-a-suite-of-new-features/), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Pricing:** Free ([Kitche.co](https://kitche.co/), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Differentiators:** Receipt-scanning to bulk-add a weekly shop; built around the WRAP £730 narrative; press credibility (BBC, ITV, Guardian); EIS / Seedrs investor profile gives a public business signal ([Republic Europe / Seedrs](https://europe.republic.com/kitche), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Maturity:** Active in 2026, no signs of decline; relaunched their app in 2023 with new features ([Kitche relaunch post](https://kitche.co/2023/01/ready-steady-kitche/), accessed 2026-05-03). I could not verify a 2026-fresh App Store update date `[low confidence]`.
- **Why it matters for FridgeCheck:** Closest direct UK competitor — same problem framing, same WRAP source, same household target. Their differentiator is *receipt scanning*; ours is barcode + AI rescue recipes.

### 3.2 NoWaste
- **URL:** <https://www.nowasteapp.com/> and <https://nowaste.ai/>
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android. Two versions exist on Play Store — `com.khcreations.nowaste` (the original) and `com.nowaste.ai` (a newer AI-branded one) which has caused user confusion ([Play Store NoWaste](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.khcreations.nowaste&hl=en_US), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Pricing:** Free; Pro at **$6.99/year** or **$29.99 lifetime** ([App Store NoWaste IAPs](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/nowaste-food-inventory-list/id926211004), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Differentiators:** Multi-location lists (fridge / freezer / pantry / spice cabinet); barcode + receipt + photo entry; cross-device sync; AI assistant ("speak or write to add food, check stock"); strong feature parity with FridgeCheck.
- **Maturity:** **4.2 / 5 stars from 743 ratings on US App Store**, last updated 6 days before access (version 7.4.6) ([App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/nowaste-food-inventory-list/id926211004), accessed 2026-05-03). Actively maintained.
- **User complaints (verified quotes):**
  > "Once in a while when you scan the barcode, the wrong expiration date pops up. There are still other times where it doesn't recognize the type of food."
  > "The app crashed and went I went back in none of the stuff I added saved. It probably only took about 5 to 10 minutes to do, but it was tedious and I was too frustrated to do it again."
  > "The app keeps putting my dry beans in my fridge for some reason. Who stores dry beans in their fridge? I can't get it to read receipts."

  ([App Store reviews](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/nowaste-food-inventory-list/id926211004), accessed 2026-05-03)
- A **breaking-change update** that forced new accounts and lost users' historical inventories caused a wave of negative reviews ([summarised across reviews on App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/nowaste-food-inventory-list/id926211004), accessed 2026-05-03). Lesson for FridgeCheck: protect migration paths obsessively.

### 3.3 Cozi
- **URL:** <https://www.cozi.com/>
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android, web.
- **Description:** Family organiser — shared calendar, shopping lists, recipe box, to-do lists. "Pantry tracking" only exists as user-improvised custom lists, not a first-class feature ([Cozi features](https://www.cozi.com/feature-overview/), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Pricing:** Free; **Cozi Gold $39/year** ([Cozi pricing analysis](https://www.usecalendara.com/blog/cozi-pricing-2026), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Differentiators:** Massive incumbency in family-organiser space; shared calendar is the centre of gravity, not pantry.
- **Maturity:** Established product (since 2005); 2024–25 reviews note "honest take after the paywall" — community has grumbled about paywalled features ([Calendara review](https://www.usecalendara.com/blog/cozi-review-2026), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Why it matters:** Competes for "household ops" attention rather than expiry tracking specifically. Threat is bundling — if Cozi added real pantry tracking, they'd already have households.

### 3.4 AnyList
- **URL:** <https://www.anylist.com/>
- **Platforms:** iOS (iPhone, iPad, Apple Watch), Android, macOS, web ([App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/anylist-grocery-shopping-list/id522167641), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Description:** Grocery list + recipe + meal plan app. Pantry tracking is supported but lightweight — users typically maintain inventory lists alongside shopping lists.
- **Pricing:** Free; **AnyList Complete $9.99/yr individual / $14.99/yr household** ([App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/anylist-grocery-shopping-list/id522167641), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Differentiators:** Best-in-class list-sharing UX, recipe import from any web URL, location-based reminders, price tracking. Loved by power users ([NerdWallet best grocery list apps 2026](https://www.nerdwallet.com/finance/learn/best-grocery-list-apps), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Maturity:** Long-running, well-reviewed; no signs of decline.
- **Why it matters:** Sets the bar for *family-shared list UX*. FridgeCheck's household model needs to feel as tight.

### 3.5 Fridgely
- **URL:** <https://fridgelyapp.com/>
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android.
- **Description:** Pantry / fridge / freezer "spaces"; barcode scan auto-fills name + estimated expiry; receipt scanning; recipes from on-hand items; family sharing ([Fridgely](https://fridgelyapp.com/), accessed 2026-05-03). Almost identical primitives to FridgeCheck.
- **Pricing:** Free + IAP (premium tier) ([App Store Fridgely](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/fridgely/id988016972), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Maturity:** **4.21 / 5 from ~160 ratings** ([AlternativeTo profile](https://alternativeto.net/software/fridgely/about/), accessed 2026-05-03) — small footprint relative to NoWaste.
- **User complaints (verified):**
  > "Every time the app updates it resets the updated dates on recently expired products" — user reports having to re-enter 150+ items.
  > "The app appears aimed at Americans despite being in the UK App Store" — manual entry needed for many UK products that don't scan ([reviews summarised across UK App Store + AppGrooves](https://appgrooves.com/compare/ios-988016972/app-nowaste-food-inventory-list-by-kh-creations-ivs), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Why it matters:** Direct feature-set twin. Their UK reception is the key signal: **a US barcode database is not enough for UK users.** Any FridgeCheck win has to rest on UK-correct product data and supermarket-aware behaviour.

### 3.6 Best Before (Peytu)
- **URL:** <https://www.peytuapp.com/best-before-food-tracker/>
- **Platforms:** iOS 17+, Android.
- **Description:** Expiry tracker with open-date tracking, family sharing, shopping lists, category/location organisation. Crowdsourced product database.
- **Pricing:** **Pay-once, no ads, no IAP** ([Peytu](https://www.peytuapp.com/best-before-food-tracker/), accessed 2026-05-03) — note the Google Play listing says "Free with ads and in-app purchases" — there's a **discrepancy** between the developer site and Play Store data `[low confidence]`.
- **Maturity:** **4.1 / 5 from 927 reviews, 50k+ installs**, last updated May 2024 ([Play Store](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.peytu.bestbefore), accessed 2026-05-03). Note: ~12 months without an update is a soft warning sign.
- **Differentiators:** Tracks "opened" date alongside expiry — important for products that have a separate post-opening shelf life (jars, sauces, juices, hummus). FridgeCheck does not have this today.
- **Common gripe:** "The barcode scanner is actually a picture you take rather than a scan of the UPC… the extra 4–5 seconds adds up quickly" ([summarised in Reddit/blog reviews](https://hydroponicglass.com/food-expiry-tracker-manage-your-fridge-and-reduce-waste/), accessed 2026-05-03). FridgeCheck wins here — ZXing is a real scanner.

### 3.7 Pantry Check
- **URL:** <https://pantrycheck.com/>
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android, web ([App Store Pantry Check](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/pantry-check-grocery-list/id966702368), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Description:** Inventory + barcode scan + expiry + smart shopping list. Made by Sunroom Labs (San Francisco).
- **Pricing:** **Free up to 200 items**; paid plans for unlimited storage; promotional Black Friday discounts of 33–50% off annual ([Pantry Check](https://pantrycheck.com/), accessed 2026-05-03). Specific price tiers not displayed on the site `[low confidence]`.
- **Differentiators:** Reputation as the *best-in-class barcode scanner*; batch barcode scanning; automatic background-removal on product photos; **users can contribute to a crowdsourced DB**.
- **Maturity:** **4.6 / 5 from 539 App Store reviews** ([Pantry Check](https://pantrycheck.com/), accessed 2026-05-03). One of the highest-rated specialist apps in this category.
- **Why it matters:** Gold standard on barcode UX — FridgeCheck should benchmark its scan-to-add flow against it.

### 3.8 KitchenPal (iCuisto)
- **URL:** <https://kitchenpalapp.com/en/>
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android.
- **Description:** Pantry + meal planner + recipes + shopping list. Premium unlocks unlimited scans, custom recipe upload, list export, custom storage sections, and family Premium.
- **Pricing:** Free; Premium tier present but **price not displayed on the homepage** ([KitchenPal](https://kitchenpalapp.com/en/), accessed 2026-05-03) `[low confidence]`.
- **Differentiators:** Premium upgrade extends to family members for free — interesting model that lowers the per-seat cost feeling for households.
- **Maturity:** Pitched/recommended by Healthline, PRI, Food Navigator. No UK or supermarket integrations.

### 3.9 Samsung Food (ex-Whisk)
- **URL:** <https://samsungfood.com/>
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android, web.
- **Description:** Recipe library (240k+ recipes), AI meal plan, "Food List" pantry, hands-free cooking mode, shoppable lists linked to (US) retailers, smart-fridge tie-in via SmartThings ([Samsung Food](https://samsungfood.com/), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Pricing:** Free; **Food+ $6.99/mo or $59.99/yr** ([Plan to Eat review](https://www.plantoeat.com/blog/2026/01/samsung-food-review-pros-and-cons/), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Differentiators:** Vertical integration with Samsung Family Hub fridges, including Bixby + Gemini AI vision food recognition (2026 CES) ([The Outpost](https://theoutpost.ai/news-story/samsung-smart-fridges-gain-voice-control-and-ai-powered-food-recognition-at-ces-2026-22730/), accessed 2026-05-03). Pantry tracking is supplementary, not the headline feature.
- **Threat:** Bundled-by-default for any Samsung-fridge household.

### 3.10 Too Good To Go
- **URL:** <https://www.toogoodtogo.com/en-gb>
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android.
- **Description:** Surplus-food marketplace — restaurants, cafés, bakeries, supermarkets sell unsold food in "magic bags" at discounts.
- **Pricing:** Free, transactional (consumer pays per bag).
- **Maturity:** **4.9★ from 1.92M Play Store reviews; 50M+ installs; 5M+ UK users; ~62M global users; ~155M bags rescued** ([Play Store TGTG](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.app.tgtg&hl=en_GB), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Too_Good_To_Go), accessed 2026-05-03). Last updated 21 April 2026.
- **UK signals:** Lincolnshire NHS hospitals signed up in 2026 to redirect catering surplus ([ULH NHS](https://www.ulh.nhs.uk/news/lincolnshire-hospitals-have-signed-up-to-too-good-to-go/), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Common Trustpilot complaints (2025):** "borderline scraps serving"; raw meat next to bread; out-of-date food being sold; customer support "days, even weeks for a single reply" ([Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/toogoodtogo.com), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Why it matters:** Different problem (rescue from retailer) but same emotional brand promise (don't waste food). TGTG owns "save food + save money" mindshare in the UK. FridgeCheck must clarify it's *complementary*, not competing.

### 3.11 Olio
- **URL:** <https://olioapp.com/en/>
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android.
- **Description:** Hyper-local food + household-item sharing. Community of "Food Waste Heroes" who collect surplus from partner stores and redistribute via the app.
- **Pricing:** Free.
- **Maturity:** **9M+ downloads; ~3M UK users; 140M+ items shared** ([Olio](https://olioapp.com/en/), [About Amazon EU](https://www.aboutamazon.eu/news/aws/olios-tessa-clarke-tackling-food-waste-with-hyper-local-community-sharing-app), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **UK retailer partnerships:** Tesco, Sainsbury's, Morrisons, Iceland, Co-operative ([Wikipedia Olio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olio_(app)), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Volunteer/community complaints:** Hoarding, volunteer dissatisfaction, suspension policies ([Olio Help Centre](https://help.olioapp.com/article/459-food-waste-hero-complaints-and-warnings), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Why it matters:** UK-native and aligned to FridgeCheck's mission. Potential partner more than competitor — their users own the same problem upstream of FridgeCheck's scope.

### 3.12 Cherrypick (UK)
- **URL:** <https://www.cherrypick.co/>
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android.
- **Description:** Recipe-to-basket meal planner — pick recipes, ingredients flow into a Tesco / Sainsbury's / Asda online basket, ready to checkout ([Cherrypick](https://www.cherrypick.co/), [The Grocer rebrand piece](https://www.thegrocer.co.uk/news/meal-planning-and-shopping-platform-lollipop-rebrands-as-cherrypick/687537.article), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Pricing:** Free with paid tiers ([The Grocer paid tiers piece](https://www.thegrocer.co.uk/news/shopping-platform-cherrypick-to-introduce-paid-for-tiers/692727.article), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Maturity:** 330k+ users; positive 2026 reviews — "this is what HelloFresh, Gusto and all the other meal prep boxes wish they thought of" ([Exposed Magazine](https://www.exposedmagazine.co.uk/featured-articles/best-meal-planning-app-with-shopping-list-the-top-uk-choices-for-2026/), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Why it matters:** Owns the *recipe → UK supermarket basket* loop. FridgeCheck's rescue-recipes flow stops at "here's a recipe" — Cherrypick continues the journey to "ingredients delivered". This is the obvious gap on FridgeCheck's side, and integrating with Tesco / Sainsbury's APIs is the moat.

### 3.13 Nosh
- **URL:** <https://nosh.tech/>
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android.
- **Description:** Barcode-scan pantry (DB of 2M+ products), expiry/best-before tracking, recipe suggestions, weekly AI analytics on waste, and a "nosh shop" for nearly-expired discounted food ([Nosh](https://nosh.tech/), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Pricing:** Free.
- **Differentiators:** **Weekly AI analytics on user's own waste patterns** — closest to a "FridgeCheck AI insight" play.
- **Maturity:** Built during COVID; smaller user base, indie-feel `[low confidence on current scale]`.

### 3.14 Mealia (UK)
- **URL:** <https://www.mealia.co.uk/>
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android, web.
- **Description:** UK meal planner with **all four major UK supermarket basket integrations** (Tesco, Asda, Sainsbury's, Morrisons) — billed as the only one to cover all four ([Mealia](https://www.mealia.co.uk/), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Pricing:** Paid (specific numbers not displayed prominently) `[low confidence]`.
- **Why it matters:** Validates demand for UK-supermarket-aware shopping experiences but solves the upstream meal-plan problem rather than the downstream expiry problem.

## 4. Synthesis

### 4.1 Common features the category has converged on (and where FridgeCheck stands)

| Convergent feature | Industry standard | FridgeCheck has it? |
|--------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Barcode scan to add | Yes — every serious pantry app | Yes (ZXing + Open Food Facts) |
| Expiry alerts / urgency tiers | Yes | Yes |
| Multiple storage locations (fridge / freezer / pantry / spice cabinet) | Yes | **No — single household scope, no per-location** |
| Receipt scanning | Yes (Kitche, NoWaste, Fridgely) | **No** |
| Recipe suggestions from on-hand items | Yes | Yes (rescue recipes, but only on the 3-in-3-days trigger) |
| Family / household sharing | Yes | Yes |
| Web app companion | Yes (AnyList, Pantry Check, Cozi, Samsung Food) | Partial — web frontend exists |
| Cross-device sync | Yes | Yes |
| Open / "use within X days of opening" tracking | Best Before, NoWaste | **No** |
| Voice entry | Kitche, NoWaste | **No** |
| AI weekly analytics on personal waste | Nosh, Kitche | **No** (we have warnings, not insight summaries) |
| Apple Watch / wearable | AnyList | **No** |

**The five cleanest "we lack" gaps vs the category:** receipt scanning, multiple storage locations, post-opening shelf-life, voice entry, and analytics summaries.

### 4.2 Standout features only one app does well

- **Pantry Check** — best-in-class barcode scan UX (batch scan, auto-photo background removal).
- **Nosh** — *weekly* AI analytics on the user's own waste pattern, not just per-item alerts.
- **Cherrypick** — recipe → UK supermarket basket → checkout in one continuous flow.
- **Olio** — hyper-local share-it-don't-bin-it network, with real UK retailer partnerships.
- **Best Before (Peytu)** — pay-once, no-subscription, no-ads model — counter-positioning vs everyone else.
- **Samsung Food** — vertical integration with the user's actual fridge hardware.
- **Kitche** — receipt-scan plus a "your waste vs national average" graph that creates a feedback loop — the closest thing to behavioural psychology in the space.

### 4.3 Verified user complaints (use as design constraints)

All quotes accessed 2026-05-03.

- **Wrong/missing barcode data:** *"Once in a while when you scan the barcode, the wrong expiration date pops up. There are still other times where it doesn't recognize the type of food."* (NoWaste, [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/nowaste-food-inventory-list/id926211004))
- **Data loss across updates:** *"every time the app updates it resets the updated dates on recently expired products"* — Fridgely user reportedly had to re-enter 150+ items ([summarised in NoWaste vs Fridgely comparison](https://appgrooves.com/compare/ios-988016972/app-nowaste-food-inventory-list-by-kh-creations-ivs), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Forced re-account on update wiping inventory:** NoWaste in 2024–25 — change "buried in update notes that auto-updating users would never see" ([App Store NoWaste reviews](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/nowaste-food-inventory-list/id926211004), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **US-bias in the product DB hurts UK users:** Fridgely UK reviewers — *"appears aimed at Americans despite being in the UK App Store"*, requiring manual entry for many products ([summarised AppGrooves](https://appgrooves.com/compare/ios-988016972/app-nowaste-food-inventory-list-by-kh-creations-ivs), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Slow scan-by-photo flow:** *"a scanner requires one action and is nearly instant, whereas a picture requires at least 3 actions, and the extra 4–5 seconds to take a picture, resize and save, add up quickly"* (Best Before, [summarised review](https://hydroponicglass.com/food-expiry-tracker-manage-your-fridge-and-reduce-waste/), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Notifications not firing on the device itself, only in-app:** NoWaste ([App Store reviews](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/nowaste-food-inventory-list/id926211004), accessed 2026-05-03).
- **Quality / safety perception in the rescue-marketplace category:** TGTG Trustpilot 2025 — raw meat alongside bread, "borderline scraps", weeks-long support response ([Trustpilot TGTG](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/toogoodtogo.com), accessed 2026-05-03).

### 4.4 Confident gaps in the market with evidence

1. **UK product database supremacy.** Fridgely's UK reviews show that even a feature-complete app fails if the product DB is US-skewed. **FridgeCheck's win condition is being the most accurate UK barcode → product → typical expiry mapping.** Open Food Facts is global; it's worth investing in a UK-tuned overlay (Tesco / Sainsbury's / Asda / Morrisons / Lidl / Aldi own-label coverage) since this is the single most cited UK-app frustration.
2. **Post-opening shelf-life tracking.** Only Best Before and partially NoWaste track "opened X days ago — use within Y". Yet a huge slice of UK home waste is opened jars, sauces, condiments. Quick win.
3. **Behavioural waste analytics.** Nosh and Kitche have rudimentary "your waste vs average" feedback. Nobody has shipped a *persuasive* weekly recap (cost, kg, CO₂, "you saved £X this week"). FridgeCheck's APScheduler worker is already in place — this is mostly a content/UX problem.
4. **Supermarket basket integration for "use what you have + buy the missing ingredients".** Cherrypick and Mealia own the upstream meal-plan → basket flow. Nobody connects it the *other* way: rescue recipe → "you have 3 of 5 ingredients, add the other 2 to your Tesco basket". Big differentiator window before Tesco's own AI assistant rolls out broadly later in 2026.
5. **No-subscription, pay-once option that targets the "I just want to track expiry" segment.** Best Before is the only mainstream option, and its Play Store listing claims ads + IAP, contradicting its own marketing. There's an honesty/trust gap any new entrant could exploit.
6. **Real iOS / Android / watch parity.** Most competitors are mobile-first with web bolted on. FridgeCheck's existing web app is actually a strategic advantage for desktop-first households, but lacks watch / widget / Siri-shortcut surface.

### 4.5 UK-specific signals worth tracking

- **Tesco AI assistant rollout (Clubcard app, 2026):** Largest direct threat for AI-recipe-from-pantry features; specifically calls out *"select meals that use leftover ingredients in the fridge or cupboard"* ([Tesco PLC](https://www.tescoplc.com/tesco-launches-large-scale-colleague-trial-of-new-ai-assistant-designed-to-lighten-the-load-for-customers/), accessed 2026-05-03).

- **Olio's Tesco / Sainsbury's / Morrisons / Iceland / Co-op partnerships** — the model for retailer integration in the UK food-waste space; useful reference if FridgeCheck pursues retailer co-marketing.
- **Cherrypick + Mealia + Tesco AI** all converging on "recipe → UK basket". A FridgeCheck strategy that ignores this loop will be irrelevant by end-2026.

## 5. What this implies for FridgeCheck

Top three product moves the evidence supports, in priority order:

1. **UK-tuned product DB + brand recognition.** Treat Open Food Facts as a starting point, layer UK retailer own-brand products on top, allow user-contributed expiry estimates that pool back to all households (Pantry Check's crowd model — but UK-specific).
2. **Post-opening shelf-life tracking + voice add.** Cheapest two industry-standard features we lack; both are weekly-shop UX wins.
3. **Tesco / Sainsbury's basket bridge for rescue recipes.** Even before deep API integrations, a "deep-link to pre-filled Tesco basket" intercept on rescue recipes would be a defensible UK-only win and a reason the user opens the app over Tesco's own assistant.

Things to avoid based on the failures observed:

- Breaking schema changes that wipe inventories (NoWaste 2024–25).
- Letting the migration story between auth or DB versions degrade.
- Marketing the £730 figure without a verified WRAP source link — the number has been superseded; update copy or cite the original 2018 source explicitly.
- Photo-based barcode capture as a primary entry path — real ZXing scanning is a differentiator, not a fallback.
